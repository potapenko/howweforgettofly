This document defines the smallest coherent version of **How We Forget to
Fly** that could later be built as an English-language interactive resource.
It specifies meaning, content, transitions, user-visible states, privacy
expectations, and acceptance criteria. It does not select a technical stack,
describe a visual design, create accounts, publish anything, or register a
domain.

The governing product thesis is simple:

> Help a parent or adult notice where a borrowed route has replaced a living
> question, make one responsible response, meet what happens, and Return
> without turning Flight into a score or identity.

The MVP is an invitation into a speculative philosophy. It is not a test of
talent, a child-development assessment, a therapeutic instrument, or a claim
that the project’s metaphors have been scientifically proven.

---

## 1. Product contract

### 1.1. What the MVP must make possible

A first-time visitor must be able to:

1. understand the project’s central proposition in plain language;
2. choose a parent, adult, or AI-assisted doorway without being classified;
3. learn only the Flight vocabulary needed for the next decision;
4. complete one small, voluntary practice that produces their own words;
5. revise or refuse every invitation that is not an actual obligation;
6. bring the result into contact with life rather than stop at reflection;
7. complete a Return and Ground step without receiving a score;
8. inspect the source and transformation boundary;
9. leave without an account, streak, profile, diagnosis, or pressure to
   continue.

### 1.2. What the MVP must never imply

The product must not imply that:

- some people are flyers and others are not;
- a parent can manufacture a creative child;
- compliance, novelty, output volume, income, or public attention measures
  human worth;
- every discomfort is a Call, or every Call must be answered;
- Compass is an inner oracle that overrides consent, evidence, duty, or care;
- Ground is failure or Gravity;
- Return requires publication, monetization, or usefulness to strangers;
- AI is neutral, a moral authority, a child’s interpreter, or the pilot of a
  human life;
- a reflective answer is a psychological, pedagogical, or medical finding;
- the source book and this project are the same work.

### 1.3. Stable world interface

The product uses the canonical cycle in this exact order:

> **Call → Compass → Lift → Making → Flight → Return → Ground**

The terms retain the meanings established in the Flight Universe Bible:

- **Call:** a noticed mismatch, possibility, or care that invites a response;
- **Compass:** human discernment of direction, limits, consent, and
  consequences;
- **Lift:** the changing conditions and available capacity for one attempt;
- **Making:** giving a question or intention a perceivable form;
- **Flight:** responsible authorship becoming an attempt that meets reality;
- **Return:** meeting other people, effects, and feedback, with something
  coming back;
- **Ground:** reality, body, craft, limits, obligations, rest, and integration.

**Gravity** names forces that quietly turn an external route or protective
habit into destiny. **Wind** names external amplifiers—especially AI—that can
increase options, resistance, speed, or reach but cannot choose a worthy
direction. A parent or caregiver is a **Keeper of Conditions**, responsible
for safety and boundaries without treating the child as a construction
project.

## 2. Audiences and entry promises

The MVP has two human audiences and one optional tool doorway. These are
visitor-chosen routes, not inferred segments.

### 2.1. Parents and other responsible caregivers

**Situation:** “I want to support a child’s authorship without abandoning my
responsibility or quietly turning the child into my project.”

**Entry promise:** learn to distinguish instruction, boundary, invitation,
co-exploration, and control; then rewrite one real family moment.

**Required dignity boundary:** the adult retains unequal practical
responsibility while the child retains equal human dignity. The site does not
ask the child to supply data and does not evaluate the child.

### 2.2. Adults beginning from their present life

**Situation:** “I have obligations, fatigue, and borrowed routes. I want to
recover one question without pretending I can start life over.”

**Entry promise:** notice one live mismatch, choose a proportionate response,
give it a small form, and learn from contact with reality.

**Required dignity boundary:** rest, routine, care work, refusal, ordinary
life, and professional help remain honorable. Remembering Flight is a
revisable practice, not a verdict about a life.

### 2.3. People choosing to work with AI

**Situation:** “I want AI to help me make, while the purpose, boundaries,
adoption, and responsibility remain human.”

**Entry promise:** assign the Wind one bounded role and keep Call and Compass
outside the instrument panel.

**Required dignity boundary:** no replacement narrative, model ranking,
child profiling, hidden automation, or claim that AI output reveals a
person’s true self.

### 2.4. A reading-only route

Every visitor can read the Home, Manifesto, pathway summaries, AI covenant,
and Source/About material without beginning an interactive experience. The
product never withholds the philosophy behind completion gates.

## 3. Minimum page inventory

The MVP consists of seven content destinations. A later implementation may
present them in different containers, but it must preserve their distinct
jobs and direct paths.

### PAGE-01 — Home: How We Forget to Fly

**Job:** recognition and voluntary orientation.

**Required content:**

- the one-sentence thesis;
- a short explanation of borrowed maps, authorship, and the age of abundant
  generated answers;
- the proposition that Flight is an event available in many forms, not a
  person type;
- three explicit choices: Parents, Adults, and Set the Wind;
- a reading-only route to the Manifesto;
- a visible route to Source/About;
- a compact refusal: no genius promise, diagnosis, score, or child profile.

**Primary actions:** choose a doorway, read the Manifesto, or inspect the
source boundary.

**Completion condition:** none. The visitor may leave from this page without
being treated as a failed conversion.

### PAGE-02 — Manifesto: What Must Remain Ours

**Job:** establish the moral and philosophical horizon.

**Required content:**

- the English Memorandum in a readable standalone form;
- a concise guide to Call, Compass, Making, Return, and Ground;
- the human-worth invariant;
- the open-myth covenant: no guru, secret knowledge, rank, compulsory belief,
  or shame;
- links into both human pathways and the AI pathway;
- a source note distinguishing inspiration, transformation, and
  project-original ideas.

**Primary actions:** continue to a pathway or stop with the text.

### PAGE-03 — Parents: Keep the Sky Open

**Job:** translate the philosophy into responsible family conduct.

**Required content:**

- “the child is not the project”;
- equal dignity and unequal responsibility;
- the Keeper of Conditions role;
- the difference among instruction, boundary, invitation, co-exploration,
  witnessing, and control;
- the child’s right to “no,” “not now,” or “differently” when the situation is
  a genuine invitation;
- clear exceptions for safety, care, law, shared obligations, and the rights
  of others;
- the Parent Covenant;
- entry to **What Grounds Flight?** and **Instruction or Invitation?**;
- a compact route to the Creative Climate Map description in the Atlas.

**Primary action:** bring one actual adult sentence or household situation,
without entering a child’s profile.

### PAGE-04 — Adults: Remembering Flight

**Job:** support one proportionate act of authorship from the visitor’s actual
life.

**Required content:**

- purpose as a practice rather than a hidden assignment;
- Ground versus Gravity;
- borrowed maps versus a living Compass;
- the complete Flight cycle;
- honorable refusal, rest, obligation, and help;
- the Adult Covenant;
- entry to **Problem Finder** and **Flight Log**;
- short descriptions of Template Escape and The Return in the Atlas.

**Primary action:** name one small mismatch or care in the visitor’s own life.

### PAGE-05 — AI: Set the Wind

**Job:** establish human-first use of an amplifier.

**Required content:**

- Wind is not wings, pilot, Compass, Call, or a measure of worth;
- six permitted roles: Mirror, Generator, Interlocutor, Critic, Craft Aid,
  and Simulator;
- the human assignments: worth, worthy Call, consent, care, Compass,
  adoption, responsibility, Return, and a child’s identity;
- the eight-step Set the Wind protocol;
- the AI Covenant;
- a path that works without sending any material to an AI system;
- entry to the **Set the Wind** guided practice.

**Primary action:** write a human-first intention and assign one bounded role.

### PAGE-06 — Practices: Interactive Atlas

**Job:** offer a non-ranked collection of voluntary experiences.

**Required content:**

- all Atlas experiences, including those not guided in the MVP;
- for each: audience, purpose, approximate kind of effort, user-authored
  output, opt-out, privacy note, and Return;
- filters only by visitor-chosen need or doorway, never by inferred trait;
- no recommended “level,” progression ladder, completion percentage, or
  mastery badge;
- seven guided `MVP-core` experiences clearly distinguished from descriptive
  `MVP-next` entries;
- a direct path to the Source/About page.

**Primary actions:** choose an experience, read about one, or leave.

### PAGE-07 — Source / About: Where the Question Began

**Job:** make provenance and genre inspectable.

**Required content:**

- the book title in Russian and a clearly labelled **project working
  rendering** in English, not an official translated title;
- a link to the preserved source URL;
- a concise account of what the source contributed;
- a concise account of what this project transformed, rejected, or created;
- the statement that this is not a full translation or neutral summary;
- the statement that the local web snapshot contains known gaps, including
  headings `t70–t80` without body text;
- the speculative-philosophy and non-diagnostic boundary;
- a route back to the Manifesto and both human pathways.

**Primary action:** inspect provenance; no belief or agreement is requested.

## 4. MVP experience selection

The full Atlas remains visible as a field of choices. The MVP guides the seven
experiences marked `MVP-core` in the Atlas because together they cover parent,
adult, AI, action, Return, and Ground without pretending to be a course.

### `EXP-01 — What Grounds Flight?`

**Audience:** parents and caregivers.  
**Purpose:** distinguish necessary responsibility from anxiety-driven control.  
**Input:** one household situation described by the adult, with no child name,
age profile, diagnosis, or history required.  
**Action:** name the adult responsibility, the result the adult may have
quietly expected, the fixed frame, the part the child can truly author, and
one repair in the adult’s own action or language.  
**User-authored output:** a three-part Ground agreement: `I will hold…`, `The
child may author…`, and `Next time I will repair or clarify…`.  
**Return:** try the sentence or action only if appropriate, observe what the
real relationship returns, then revise.  
**Ground:** close the situation, restore the shared space, seek help, or simply
stop.  
**No-answer path:** “I cannot distinguish these yet” returns a small offline
observation prompt, not a result.

### `EXP-03 — Instruction or Invitation?`

**Audience:** parents and caregivers.  
**Purpose:** make the adult’s actual authority and the child’s actual choice
legible.  
**Input:** one sentence the adult is considering saying.  
**Action:** name what is actually required, what is genuinely open, and whether
`no` can survive; then choose exactly one honest mode—direct instruction,
boundary with choice, genuine invitation, co-exploring, or witnessing—and
rewrite the sentence so its grammar matches the truth.  
**User-authored output:** the original sentence, chosen mode, revised sentence,
one behaviour needed to honour that mode, and a check for whether “no” is a
real answer.  
**Return:** use the revision only in a suitable moment and listen for effects,
not compliance alone.  
**Ground:** accept that direct instruction can be caring when it is honestly
named and proportionate.  
**Refusal path:** the adult can keep the original sentence and leave with the
distinction alone.

### `EXP-02 — Problem Finder`

**Audience:** adults.  
**Purpose:** turn a small live mismatch into a question without declaring it a
destiny.  
**Input:** one irritation, care, curiosity, or recurring friction.  
**Action:** describe what is happening, who is affected, what remains unknown,
what might be obligation rather than Call, and whether the present response is
`yes`, `no`, `not now`, or `differently`.  
**User-authored output:** one possible Call and one present response. A small
form may be offered only as an optional next step after `yes` or `differently`;
it is not part of the required result.  
**Return:** if the visitor chooses to continue, meet one person, material,
place, or constraint touched by the question.  
**Ground:** record what became clearer, rest, return to duty, or retire the
question without shame.  
**No-Call path:** “Nothing is asking me now” is a complete and honorable state.

### `EXP-05 — AI as Wind: Set the Wind`

**Audience:** adults or parents using AI for their own thinking and making.  
**Purpose:** use AI in one explicit role while keeping direction, adoption, and
responsibility human.  
**Input:** a human-written Call note and Compass note.  
**Action:** choose one role, state material and privacy boundaries, formulate
one request, inspect the response for drift, and perform an adoption pass.  
**User-authored output:** a bounded request plus a record of what was adopted,
changed, rejected, or remains uncertain.  
**Return:** test the adopted form against reality and affected people.  
**Ground:** close the tool, retain responsibility, and note any attribution
owed.  
**No-AI path:** the same steps can be completed as a planning card without
sending the request anywhere.

### `EXP-06 — Flight Log`

**Audience:** parents reflecting on their own action and adults reflecting on
their own attempt.  
**Purpose:** hold one cycle without turning it into a streak or performance
record.  
**Input:** a visitor-owned question, attempt, or family action.  
**Action:** write a short note for Call, Compass, Lift, Making, Flight, Return,
and Ground; skip any stage that would require invention.  
**User-authored output:** one complete or intentionally incomplete cycle note.  
**Return:** name what or who was met and what came back; “no external Return
yet” remains valid.  
**Ground:** choose close, rest, repair, continue later, or retire.  
**Exit path:** the visitor can copy, export in a plain human-readable form if
offered later, discard, or leave without saving.

### `EXP-08 — The Return`

**Audience:** shared; used after a form, sentence, experiment, or decision has
met reality or affected another person.  
**Purpose:** notice effects and choose a fitting response without turning
Return into publicity, gratitude, or impact rank.  
**Input:** one encounter, consequence, response, silence, surprise, or harm.  
**Action:** distinguish what happened from what was hoped for; name who or what
was affected; choose learning, value, repair, privacy, revision, or an honest
ending.  
**User-authored output:** a Return note containing only what the visitor can
actually observe and one response they freely adopt.  
**Return:** this experience is the explicit Return stage; it may remain private
unless responsibility requires an addressed repair or response.  
**Ground:** continue to `EXP-09`, revise the form, perform a concrete repair,
or stop.  
**No-contact path:** “This has not met anyone or anything yet” is valid and
does not trigger a demand to publish.

### `EXP-09 — Ground Landing`

**Audience:** shared; available from any pathway or experience.  
**Purpose:** close, pause, integrate, rest, ask for help, or retire a cycle
without debt to continue.  
**Input:** any completed, incomplete, refused, interrupted, or uncertain
attempt.  
**Action:** identify what now needs closing, care, repair, practice, rest,
support, storage, disposal, or no further action.  
**User-authored output:** an optional landing sentence and a chosen disposition
for the current material.  
**Return:** if an obligation or repair remains, it is named plainly; otherwise
Ground does not invent one.  
**Ground:** this experience is the explicit Ground stage and has no automatic
continuation.  
**Immediate-exit path:** the visitor may leave without writing a landing
sentence or choosing another experience.

### 4.1. Why the `MVP-next` experiences remain in the Atlas

**Creative Climate Map**, **Template Escape**, and **Borrowed Map, Living
Compass** remain fully described Atlas entries and may be completed without a
guided interface. Their ideas are also present inside the MVP-core: conditions
appear in `EXP-01`, while borrowed routes can surface in `EXP-02`. They are
future guided candidates, not locked levels.

No later experience is “advanced.” Adding an experience expands the available
language; it does not create an altitude ladder.

## 5. Shared interaction grammar

Every guided experience follows the same humane grammar. A visitor may move
backward, skip a nonessential prompt, revise, or leave.

### S0 — Orientation

The visitor sees what the experience is for, what it produces, what it does
not evaluate, what information it asks for, and whether an offline/no-AI route
exists.

Available actions: begin, read the concept, choose another experience, or
leave.

### S1 — Voluntary consent

The visitor confirms only that they want to try the reflection. This is not
legal consent, a terms gate, or agreement with the philosophy.

Available actions: continue or decline. Declining produces no warning,
countdown, lost-progress message, or persuasion.

### S2 — Call or situation note

The visitor names a situation in their own words. Prompts offer examples but
do not complete the answer or infer a trait.

Valid states include:

- a concrete note;
- “I do not know”;
- “nothing is calling now”;
- “this is an obligation, not an invitation”;
- blank, followed by leave or a generic example.

### S3 — Compass and boundary

The visitor considers people affected, consent, duties, available evidence,
risks, limits, and what must remain human. The experience does not announce a
morally correct answer.

### S4 — Lift and proportion

The visitor sizes the next action to actual time, energy, skill, material,
support, and uncertainty. “Rest,” “ask for help,” and “not now” are first-class
outcomes.

### S5 — Making

The visitor creates a sentence, question, request, boundary, tiny form, or
attempt plan. The product labels it as the visitor’s draft, not as a generated
finding.

### S6 — Flight

The product asks what contact with reality is proportionate. It never rewards
publication, exposure, or scale over private, relational, or local action.

### S7 — Return

The visitor records effects, responses, obligations, surprise, resistance,
or the absence of contact. Return is relational and consequential, not a duty
to perform usefulness.

### S8 — Ground

The visitor chooses close, rest, care, repair, practice, continue later, or
retire. The experience ends without a score, diagnosis, celebratory rank,
streak, or coercive next step.

## 6. Required user-visible states

### 6.1. First visit

- No account wall or questionnaire appears before the thesis.
- The parent doorway appears first, while the adult doorway is visibly equal
  in dignity and complete in scope.
- The visitor can identify the speculative genre and non-diagnostic boundary
  before entering a practice.

### 6.2. Returning visit

- The product assumes no personal history and starts fresh.
- A returning visitor does not need to delete or clear a profile because the
  MVP has no saved reflection history.
- No streak, absence warning, progress percentage, or “welcome back to your
  journey” inference is required.

### 6.3. Blank or uncertain answer

- Blank is not interpreted as resistance, low creativity, or incapacity.
- The visitor can see a neutral fictional example, write “not sure,” skip when
  safe, or leave.
- The product does not fabricate a completed answer in the visitor’s voice.

### 6.4. Refusal

- “No,” “not now,” and “differently” are complete outcomes for invitations.
- A family practice must still distinguish refusal from unavoidable safety,
  care, legal, and shared obligations.
- Refusal never triggers shame copy, a lowered rating, or repeated prompts.

### 6.5. Insufficient Lift

- The visitor can reduce the action, seek conditions, rest, ask for help,
  perform an obligation only, or stop.
- The state is not labelled lost motivation or failure.

### 6.6. AI unavailable or unwanted

- The AI page and Set the Wind experience remain readable and usable as a
  human planning protocol.
- No critical idea depends on a live model response.
- The product does not pressure the visitor to paste private family material
  into an external system.

### 6.7. AI output drifts from direction

- The visitor can mark material adopt, revise, reject, or uncertain.
- The product returns to the human-written Compass note.
- It does not optimize the prompt on the visitor’s behalf until the visitor
  chooses a new bounded role or request.

### 6.8. No Return yet

- The visitor can record that the form has not met another person or reality.
- Suggested outcomes are: choose one safe contact, keep it private for now,
  return later, or close it.
- The product does not equate audience size with Return.

### 6.9. Error or interruption

- The visitor receives a short, plain statement that the current step could
  not continue.
- If their words remain available in the current session, that fact is stated;
  if not, the loss is stated without pretending recovery.
- The next choices are retry, copy what is available, return to the Atlas, or
  leave.
- No error state sends visitor-authored text to a new service without explicit
  action.

### 6.10. Completion

- Completion means the visitor reached a chosen stopping point, not that the
  person passed the practice.
- The summary contains only visitor-authored or clearly labelled example
  material.
- The final choices are copy/retain if available, discard, choose another
  experience, read related material, or leave.
- Any next-experience suggestion is optional and never described as the next
  level.

## 7. Navigation and transitions

The navigation must preserve choice without making the site feel like a
curriculum.

### 7.1. Required global routes

Every destination provides a direct route to:

- Home;
- Manifesto;
- Parents;
- Adults;
- AI / Set the Wind;
- Interactive Atlas;
- Source / About.

The exact presentation is a later design decision. The semantic destinations
and their labels must remain understandable without mythic vocabulary.

### 7.2. Required contextual transitions

| From | Offered transition | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Home | Parent, Adult, AI, Manifesto, Source | visitor chooses the job |
| Manifesto | Parent or Adult; optional AI | translate doctrine into a life |
| Parents | `EXP-01` or `EXP-03` | move from idea to one adult action |
| Adults | `EXP-02` or `EXP-06` | move from recognition to one small form |
| AI | `EXP-05` or no-AI protocol | configure an amplifier without surrendering direction |
| Any experience | Return, Ground, Atlas, pathway, leave | complete without capture |
| Atlas | any entry, pathways, Source | preserve non-ranked exploration |
| Source / About | Manifesto, Parents, Adults | return from provenance to the living project |

### 7.3. Parent path acceptance journey

Home → Parents → choose a real adult situation → What Grounds Flight? → state
responsibility and the child’s remaining authorship → try or decline the new
action → Return → Ground.

The path passes only if the adult can choose direct instruction where honest,
can acknowledge a genuine “no,” and receives no judgment of the child or
parent.

### 7.4. Adult path acceptance journey

Home → Adults → Problem Finder → distinguish Call from duty or noise → size to
current Lift → make one revisable question/form → meet reality or choose not
yet → Return → Ground.

The path passes only if “nothing now,” rest, obligation, and retirement remain
valid outcomes.

### 7.5. AI path acceptance journey

Home or either human pathway → AI → write Call and Compass before assistance →
choose one Wind role → set boundaries → formulate one request or use no-AI
mode → adoption pass → reality contact → Return → Ground.

The path passes only if no output is framed as the user’s true direction and
the user can reject all of it without losing their own starting notes.

## 9. Data, privacy, and saving contract

This section specifies user expectations, not a storage architecture.

### 9.1. Default posture

- The philosophy and practices are usable without an account.
- The product does not create a child profile.
- The product does not ask for a child’s name, exact age, diagnosis,
  educational record, images, recordings, location, or contact information.
- Family examples are written by the responsible adult about the adult’s own
  decision and may use fictional labels.
- Reflective answers are not converted into scores, inferred traits,
  recommendations about a child, advertising segments, or public content.
- No practice requires cloud persistence in order to be meaningful.

### 9.2. MVP saving mode

The MVP has no saved reflection history. Answers may exist ephemerally during
the active session so the visitor can revise them. The visitor may explicitly
copy, print, or export a human-readable artifact under their own control, or
discard it. Returning to the resource starts a fresh session; the resource
does not reconstruct a previous cycle from retained content.

Local draft persistence, remote accounts, synchronized journals, analytics on
reflective text, and server-side profiles are outside the MVP. Adding any of
them requires a new privacy decision, threat review, and product scope. A
future proposal may not quietly reinterpret explicit export as permission for
the resource to keep a history.

### 9.3. AI boundary

The documentary MVP does not require a live AI integration. If a future build
offers one, it must disclose before submission:

- that text will leave the local resource;
- which service receives it;
- what should not be included;
- whether it is retained;
- how to use the full no-AI version instead.

The product must not send a child’s material, infer a child’s identity, or
quietly use visitor text to personalize later judgments.

## 10. Content accessibility and agency

Accessibility here is a meaning requirement before it is a design or
technical implementation task.

- All governing ideas are available as text outside the guided practices.
- Mythic language is paired with plain-language explanation on first use.
- No essential instruction depends only on animation, sound, color, dragging,
  timing, or spatial metaphor.
- A visitor can move through an experience without a countdown or speed
  requirement.
- Prompts use short sections and disclose the expected output before asking
  for personal reflection.
- Examples are explicitly fictional and can be hidden or ignored.
- The site does not use failure copy for blank, refusal, rest, or exit.
- English is the MVP product language; the Russian Memorandum is an editorial
  meaning reference, not a requirement for the first public surface.
- The source title may remain in Russian with an explanatory English rendering.
- Professional, medical, psychological, legal, and safeguarding support is
  named as outside the metaphor when relevant; the product does not obstruct
  seeking it.

## 11. Acceptance criteria by page

### AC-PAGE-01 — Home

- A first-time visitor can state the project’s promise after reading one
  screen-equivalent of content, without needing the complete vocabulary.
- Parent, Adult, AI, Manifesto, and Source routes are all findable.

## 12. End-to-end acceptance scenarios

### E2E-01 — A parent chooses honest instruction

Given a visitor enters a safety situation, when they use What Grounds Flight?,
then the product permits a clear adult instruction, helps preserve only the
choice that is real, and produces no negative judgment about creativity.

### E2E-02 — A parent discovers a disguised command

Given a visitor enters a sentence posed as a question, when they use
Instruction or Invitation?, then they can either name the non-negotiable
boundary honestly or make “no” a genuine answer. The product does not insist
that invitation is always morally superior.

### E2E-03 — An adult has no available Lift

Given a visitor names a live concern but no current capacity, when they reach
Lift, then rest, asking for help, fulfilling an obligation only, or “not now”
can complete the session without failure language.

### E2E-04 — An adult finds no Call

Given a visitor does not feel a live question, when they use Problem Finder,
then “nothing is asking me now” is accepted without an inferred deficit or a
forced suggestion.

### E2E-05 — AI proposes the destination

Given an AI response reframes the goal, when the visitor performs the adoption
pass, then they can reject the reframing and return to their human-written
Call and Compass. The product does not call the AI’s proposal insight into the
visitor’s true purpose.

### E2E-06 — A visitor uses no AI

Given a visitor chooses Set the Wind but will not send material to a model,
when they select the no-AI path, then they can complete the role, boundary,
request, and adoption planning card with the same philosophical value.

### E2E-07 — Return remains private

Given a visitor makes something only for one relationship or for their own
integration, when they reach Return, then the product accepts the encounter
and consequences without requiring publication, market value, or audience.

### E2E-08 — A visitor leaves midway

Given a visitor exits from any prompt, when they leave, then no shame,
countdown, streak loss, or diagnostic conclusion appears. The saving behavior
matches the disclosed mode.

### E2E-09 — A visitor inspects provenance first

Given a visitor begins at Source/About, when they review the project’s origin,
then they can distinguish source paraphrase, project transformation, and
project-origin doctrine, and continue directly to the Manifesto or a human
pathway.
