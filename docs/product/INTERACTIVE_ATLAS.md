## 1. What an interactive experience means here

An interactive experience in **How We Forget to Fly** is a bounded invitation
that helps a visitor author a reflection, sentence, boundary, decision, small
experiment, or ending. It does not discover a truth about the visitor. It does
not evaluate a child. It does not convert answers into a score, type,
diagnosis, prediction, recommendation about talent, or measure of readiness to
fly.

The atlas turns the project’s philosophy into actions small enough to meet
ordinary life. An experience may help a person:

- notice a possible Call without being commanded to answer it;
- distinguish Ground from Gravity;
- make a boundary or invitation more honest;
- choose a direction without demanding certainty;
- give a question one revisable form;
- set AI a bounded role;
- meet feedback and consequences;
- make a fitting Return;
- land, ask for help, stop, or leave with nothing recorded.

The governing invariant is `AX-12`: no answer, refusal, pause, use of help,
output, originality, usefulness, or completion state changes anyone’s human
worth. Flight is an event in a situation, never a class of person.

The masterbrand remains **How We Forget to Fly**. **Keep the Sky Open** may
name the parent path, and **Remembering Flight** may name the adult path. These
are doorways into one resource, not separate brands or levels.

## 2. Participation and safety contract

Every experience in this atlas follows the same contract:

1. It begins as an invitation. `No`, `not now`, `differently`, pause, and exit
   are complete responses.
2. It asks about one situation or cycle, not a whole personality, childhood,
   family, or life.
3. It produces only a user-authored artifact. The resource does not infer a
   hidden meaning from the response.
4. It has no score, level, ideal answer, progress rank, or comparison with
   another visitor.
5. It does not diagnose talent, creativity, parenting, motivation, health, or
   psychological state.
6. Parent-facing experiences examine the adult’s frame, language, and
   responsibility. They do not create a profile of the child.
7. The resource does not retain children’s names, ages, schools, contact
   details, work samples, behavioural histories, or other identifying data.
   The conceptual model contains no child record.
8. A visitor may keep an artifact private, copy it into their own materials,
   discard it, or leave before creating it.
9. Immediate safety, consent, real obligations, and appropriate outside help
   take priority over completing an experience or preserving a metaphor.
10. Return may be private, addressed, reparative, or an honest ending. It does
    not require publication, praise, market value, gratitude, or proof of
    impact.

If an experience concerns immediate danger, the visitor should leave the
reflection and act in plain language. The Flight metaphor is not a substitute
for medical, psychological, educational, legal, safeguarding, or other
professional judgment.

## 4. Experience catalogue

### `EXP-01 — What Grounds Flight?`

**Audience and job.** A parent or other responsible adult reflecting on one
recurring family moment. The job is to separate what the adult must genuinely
hold from what the child can truly author.

**Governing terms and axioms.** `TERM-02 Ground`, `TERM-03 Gravity`, `TERM-06
Compass`, `TERM-10 Keeper of Conditions`; `AX-05`, `AX-06`, `AX-07`, `AX-12`;
`PH-06`; exclusions `RT-02` and `RT-07`.

**Intended insight.** A clear frame and a living choice can coexist. Safety,
time, cost, care, and shared-space limits are not automatically control. A
hidden preferred result does not become the child’s Call merely because it is
expressed gently.

**Invitation and action.** Choose one visible family situation after it has
passed—not during danger. Describe what happened without trait words. Name the
adult responsibility, a possible unexamined pull, the fixed frame, the open
part, and one repair for next time.

**Minimal flow.**

1. Complete: “What I could observe was….”
2. Choose the adult responsibility involved: safety, care, shared use, time,
   cost, consent, or another plainly named duty.
3. Complete: “A result I may have quietly expected was….” The visitor may skip
   this line.
4. Complete: “What I must hold is…” and “What the child can truly author is….”
5. Write one next-time repair involving the adult’s action or language.

**User-authored output.** A three-part Ground agreement:

> I will hold…  
> The child may author…  
> Next time I will repair or clarify…

It is a note about the adult’s frame in one moment, not a description of the
child.

**Opt-out and exit.** The visitor may use a fictional example, skip the hidden
expectation prompt, discard the note, or leave with the two-line distinction
only. In immediate danger, exit and act; reflection can happen later or never.

**No-score rule.** The resource must not rate the parent, the amount of choice,
the family climate, or the child’s response. It must not count “open” choices
as evidence of better parenting.

**Misuse or failure mode.** The experience fails if it becomes a way to prove
that a child is difficult, certify the adult as enlightened, weaken a necessary
safety boundary, or offer a false choice whose unwanted answer will be
punished.

**Safety and privacy.** Prompts explicitly ask the adult not to enter a child’s
name, age, school, diagnosis, image, work sample, or identifying story. Nothing
in the output is retained as a child record. Concrete safety and safeguarding
concerns leave the experience and go to appropriate real-world action.

**Return and next path.** The Return is one clearer frame or repair. The visitor
may continue to `EXP-03 Instruction or Invitation?`, land through `EXP-09`, or
leave.

**MVP tier.** `MVP-core` — it is the clearest parent doorway and directly
expresses “equal dignity, unequal responsibility.”

### `EXP-02 — Problem Finder`

**Audience and job.** An adult with a vague irritation, care, curiosity, or
unfinished possibility. A parent may also use it for their own question, never
to extract a Call from a child. The job is to form one possible question or an
honest non-answer.

**Governing terms and axioms.** `TERM-07 The Call`, `TERM-06 Compass`, `TERM-02
Ground`; `AX-03`, `AX-05`, `AX-08`, `AX-12`; `PH-01`, `PH-03`; exclusion
`RT-06`.

**Intended insight.** A mismatch may become a Call, but it is not a command,
symptom, destiny, or obligation to create. Attention can end in `yes`, `no`,
`not now`, or `differently`.

**Invitation and action.** Notice one thing without immediately solving it.
Move from observation to care, then decide whether a response belongs to this
person now.

**Minimal flow.**

1. Complete: “I notice….”
2. Complete or skip: “I care because….”
3. Ask: “Whose question might this be?”
4. Draft: “A possible question is….”
5. Choose `yes`, `no`, `not now`, or `differently`, with an optional reason.

**User-authored output.** One possible Call and one present response. A valid
output is: “I notice this, but it is not my question to answer.”

**Opt-out and exit.** The visitor may stop after observation, decline to name a
reason, turn the prompt into a private thought, discard the text, or choose no
Call.

**No-score rule.** The resource must not count problems noticed, favour `yes`
over `no`, interpret specificity as readiness, or rank questions by originality
or social usefulness.

**Misuse or failure mode.** The experience fails if every discomfort is turned
into a project, distress is romanticized as a Call, the visitor is pressed to
act, or the output is interpreted as a life purpose.

**Safety and privacy.** No personal disclosure is required. The prompt should
allow generic wording. Pain, danger, exhaustion, or crisis may call for Ground
or outside help rather than a creative task; the experience makes no judgment
about which.

**Return and next path.** If the visitor chooses `yes` or `differently`, they
may continue to `EXP-10 Borrowed Map, Living Compass`, `EXP-04 Template Escape`,
or an independent one-day form. If they choose `no` or `not now`, they may use
`EXP-09 Ground Landing` or simply leave.

**MVP tier.** `MVP-core` — it is the simplest adult doorway and establishes the
right to decline before any making begins.

### `EXP-03 — Instruction or Invitation?`

**Audience and job.** A parent or responsible adult preparing for or repairing
one sentence in a family situation. The job is to name whether the adult is
giving a direct instruction, holding a boundary with choice, making a genuine
invitation, co-exploring, or witnessing.

**Governing terms and axioms.** `TERM-10 Keeper of Conditions`, `TERM-07 The
Call`, `TERM-06 Compass`, `TERM-02 Ground`; `AX-03`, `AX-05`, `AX-07`, `AX-12`;
`PH-06`; exclusions `RT-02` and `RT-07`.

**Intended insight.** Gentle grammar does not make a demand voluntary. An
invitation is real only if refusal can survive; a necessary instruction can be
respectful when it is named honestly.

**Invitation and action.** Bring one sentence the adult often says, with names
and identifying details removed. Identify what is required, why, what is
genuinely open, and whether `no` can be accepted. Rewrite the sentence in its
honest mode.

**Minimal flow.**

1. Write the sentence without identifying details.
2. Complete: “What is actually required is…” and “It is required because….”
3. Complete: “What is genuinely open is….”
4. Answer: “Can `no` survive without lost affection, status, or later access?”
5. Choose the honest mode and write one new sentence plus one behaviour needed
   to honour it.

**User-authored output.** A mode, an honest sentence, and a behavioural
commitment. Example: “Boundary with choice: the table must be clear by six;
the storage method is open; I will accept any safe method.”

**Opt-out and exit.** The visitor may work with a fictional sentence, keep the
rewrite private, stop after naming the mode, or decide that direct instruction
is the honest answer.

**No-score rule.** No mode is inherently higher. The resource must not award
more value to invitations, count choices, or describe a parent as controlling,
permissive, creative, or uncreative.

**Misuse or failure mode.** The experience fails if it teaches softer
manipulation, disguises an unavoidable requirement as choice, makes safety
negotiable in order to sound open, or treats a child’s `no` as data to overcome.

**Safety and privacy.** In immediate danger, direct action comes first; the
experience is not used in the moment. No child identity or behavioural history
is collected. The adult remains responsible for consequences that a child
cannot hold.

**Return and next path.** The Return is using the sentence once, then noticing
whether the adult honoured its stated openness. The visitor may continue to
`EXP-01 What Grounds Flight?`, `EXP-07 Creative Climate Map`, `EXP-06 Flight
Log`, or `EXP-09 Ground Landing`.

**MVP tier.** `MVP-core` — it converts a central parent principle into an
immediately usable, dignity-preserving artifact.

### `EXP-04 — Template Escape`

**Audience and job.** An adult—or a family member working only on their own
action—who notices a familiar template but does not need to reject the whole
routine. The job is to test one small, reversible variation.

**Governing terms and axioms.** `TERM-03 Gravity`, `TERM-06 Compass`, `TERM-05
Making`, `TERM-02 Ground`; `AX-04`, `AX-06`, `AX-08`, `AX-10`, `AX-12`;
`PH-04`, `PH-09`.

**Intended insight.** Authorship does not require rebellion, total novelty, or
the destruction of routine. Changing one variable can reveal which part of a
form is useful Ground and which part has become an unquestioned route.

**Invitation and action.** Select one repeated task, method, or format. Name
what it protects or makes possible. Change one variable—order, scale, material,
audience, location, question, or definition of done—and run one bounded test.

**Minimal flow.**

1. Name the template and the useful purpose it may carry.
2. Name one assumption that currently goes unexamined.
3. Choose one variable to change and one boundary to keep.
4. Define a reversible encounter possible in one day or less and a stop point.
5. Afterward, record what the variation revealed; keep, adapt, or end it.

**User-authored output.** A Template Escape card containing the original
template, retained purpose, one variation, one boundary, one stop point, and
one observation.

**Opt-out and exit.** The visitor may decide that the existing template is
useful, decline the experiment, rehearse it only in imagination, or stop before
the encounter.

**No-score rule.** The variation is not rated for novelty, courage, efficiency,
or success. Keeping the original template is not a lower outcome.

**Misuse or failure mode.** The experience fails if difference becomes a moral
good, real obligations are treated as disposable, risk is romanticized, or
routine work and people who prefer it are demeaned.

**Safety and privacy.** The test must respect consent, safety, employment or
family obligations, and other people’s property and privacy. Prompts ask for
role labels rather than names. A high-consequence change belongs outside this
small experiment.

**Return and next path.** The visitor may record the encounter in `EXP-06
Flight Log`, use `EXP-08 The Return` if others were affected, or close with
`EXP-09 Ground Landing`.

**MVP tier.** `MVP-next` — valuable for adult activation, but it should follow
comprehension testing that confirms “escape” is not heard as contempt for
routine, relationships, or limits.

### `EXP-05 — AI as Wind: Set the Wind`

**Audience and job.** An adult or parent before and after optional use of an
external AI system. The job is to name the human question, give AI one bounded
role, protect consent and privacy, and make adoption visible.

**Governing terms and axioms.** `TERM-08 Wind`, `TERM-06 Compass`, `TERM-05
Making`, `TERM-09 Return`; `AX-04`, `AX-05`, `AX-09`, `AX-10`, `AX-12`;
`PH-07`; exclusions `RT-08` and `RT-09`.

**Intended insight.** AI changes which forms and routes are easy, visible, and
convincing. It can assist thought and craft without receiving authority to
assign purpose, give consent, judge human worth, or absorb responsibility.

**Invitation and action.** Before using AI, write a short Wind agreement. If
the visitor later uses an external tool, return to record what was accepted,
changed, or rejected. The experience itself does not need to generate content
or call an AI system.

**Minimal flow.**

1. Complete: “The human question or task is….” `I do not know yet` is valid.
2. Choose one AI role: options, questions, critique, organization, rehearsal,
   or craft assistance—or choose no AI.
3. Name decisions not delegated: purpose, personal disclosure, consent,
   factual adoption, moral judgment, final form, publication, or another
   explicit boundary.
4. State what information must not be entered.
5. After external use, summarize rather than import the response; mark relevant
   suggestions `accept`, `change`, or `reject` and say why.
6. Name who remains responsible for the adopted form and its Return.

**User-authored output.** A Wind agreement and adoption note:

> My question…  
> Wind’s role…  
> Decisions kept human…  
> Information kept private…  
> What I accepted, changed, or rejected…  
> What I remain responsible for…

**Opt-out and exit.** The visitor may choose no AI, complete only the pre-use
reflection, use a hypothetical output, stop external use, reject everything,
or leave without justifying the choice.

**No-score rule.** There is no authorship percentage, AI-dependence score,
purity rating, originality judgment, or comparison between human and machine
performance. Using AI is not lower; refusing it is not purer.

**Misuse or failure mode.** The experience fails if the checklist becomes
automatic absolution, the resource asks AI to discover the visitor’s true Call,
machine fluency becomes evidence of a worthy direction, or a parent uses AI to
interpret a child’s personality or future.

**Safety and privacy.** Do not enter a child’s identifying information, private
work, voice, image, school details, health information, or another person’s
confidential material into this experience or an external AI tool without an
appropriate, informed basis and permission. High-stakes decisions remain with
responsible people and relevant professionals; a generated answer does not
change that duty.

**Return and next path.** The visitor may carry the adoption note into `EXP-06
Flight Log`, meet effects through `EXP-08 The Return`, revise the Wind role, or
end with `EXP-09 Ground Landing`.

**MVP tier.** `MVP-core` — it is the project’s distinct contemporary
contribution and can work as a reflective worksheet without embedding an AI
service or collecting source material.

### `EXP-06 — Flight Log`

**Audience and job.** Any adult recording one personal cycle or one adult-held
family frame. The job is to remember the relationship among question, choice,
conditions, form, encounter, consequences, and Ground without building a
record of personal performance.

**Governing terms and axioms.** The full cycle `Call → Compass → Lift → Making
→ Flight → Return → Ground`; `AX-02`, `AX-03`, `AX-05`, `AX-06`, `AX-08`,
`AX-11`, `AX-12`; `PH-01`, `PH-03`, `PH-09`; `META-01`, `META-03`.

**Intended insight.** A cycle can become legible without becoming an identity.
A pause, refusal, request for help, repair, or honest ending belongs in the log
without being counted as failure.

**Invitation and action.** Choose one current, completed, paused, or declined
cycle. Fill only the fields that help. The log may begin at any stage and may
end before all stages are present.

**Minimal flow.**

1. **Call:** What did I notice, and did I choose it?
2. **Compass:** Why did I choose, refuse, or revise it? Who was affected and
   what boundary mattered?
3. **Lift:** What capacity, conditions, or help were present or absent?
4. **Making / Flight:** What form, if any, met material, a person, a place, or
   a fact?
5. **Feedback / Return:** What answered back, and what did I offer, learn,
   repair, keep private, or honestly end?
6. **Ground:** What now needs care, closure, help, rest, or nothing further?

**User-authored output.** One editable cycle note. The user may finish it with
`complete`, `paused`, `ended`, `kept private`, `not now`, or their own words.
These are descriptions chosen by the user, not system statuses or grades.

**Opt-out and exit.** Every field is optional. The visitor may keep the cycle
entirely in mind, copy only one line, discard the note, stop logging, or leave
without a closing label.

**No-score rule.** No streaks, totals, completion percentages, cycle counts,
altitude, speed, consistency rating, sentiment score, profile, or comparison.
The resource must not turn multiple logs into a trend about a person or family.

**Misuse or failure mode.** The experience fails if it becomes a productivity
tracker, a portfolio proving creativity, a surveillance log about a child, a
parenting compliance record, or a reason to manufacture another Call.

**Safety and privacy.** The conceptual MVP requires no account and no retained
reflection history. A parent’s note concerns the adult’s choices and generic
context, not a child record. Prompts discourage names and identifying details.
Any later proposal for saved histories requires a separate privacy decision
outside this atlas; child histories remain prohibited.

**Return and next path.** The visitor may choose `EXP-08 The Return`, `EXP-09
Ground Landing`, revise an earlier stage, or end. The log never automatically
starts or recommends a new cycle.

**MVP tier.** `MVP-core` — it is the shared spine across parent, adult, and AI
paths, provided it remains single-cycle, editable, non-retained, and unscored.

### `EXP-07 — Creative Climate Map`

**Audience and job.** A parent examining the conditions around one specific,
optional attempt. An adult may adapt it for their own conditions. The job is
to choose one condition worth supporting, not to assess a person or household.

**Governing terms and axioms.** `TERM-02 Ground`, `TERM-04 Lift`, `TERM-10
Keeper of Conditions`; `AX-06`, `AX-07`, `AX-12`; `PH-05`, `PH-06`; exclusions
`RT-02`, `RT-07`, and `RT-10`.

**Intended insight.** Conditions change what is possible here and now. Their
presence or absence says nothing about talent, character, family quality, or
future potential. The child may not want the attempt even when conditions are
available.

**Invitation and action.** Choose one situation, not “the child’s creativity.”
Look at safety, time, rest, materials, freedom of form, access to craft,
conversation, and a possible Return. Use descriptive words, choose one
condition to change, and leave the rest alone.

**Minimal flow.**

1. Name one attempt in generic terms and ask whether it is wanted at all.
2. For each of eight conditions, choose or write a description such as `enough
   for this attempt`, `strained`, `missing`, or `not relevant`.
3. Do not total the descriptions. Choose one condition most worth changing.
4. Name one condition already sufficient and leave it alone.
5. Name what the adult must provide and what remains the child’s choice.
6. Write one small condition experiment or choose no change.

**User-authored output.** A one-situation climate note: one condition to
support, one condition to leave alone, one adult responsibility, one genuine
choice, and the option of no attempt.

**Opt-out and exit.** The visitor may skip any condition, use a hypothetical
situation, stop after naming what is already enough, choose no change, or
discard the map. The child does not have to participate or agree to be mapped.

**No-score rule.** No totals, averages, radar charts, traffic-light colours,
health indicators, readiness percentages, comparisons, “ideal climate,” or
longitudinal trend. Descriptive words must never be converted into numbers.

**Misuse or failure mode.** The experience fails if it becomes a family audit,
labels a child under-motivated, treats low Lift as low worth, blames a parent
for structural limits, optimizes every free moment, or uses available
conditions to create an obligation to perform.

**Safety and privacy.** The model has no child entity and stores no recurring
map. The adult is prompted not to enter names, diagnoses, school records, or
identifying circumstances. Concrete lack of safety or support is not solved by
a label; it may require material or professional help beyond the experience.

**Return and next path.** The Return is one changed condition, one condition
consciously left alone, or the discovery that no attempt is wanted. Optional
next paths are `EXP-01 What Grounds Flight?`, `EXP-03 Instruction or
Invitation?`, `EXP-09 Ground Landing`, or exit.

**MVP tier.** `MVP-next` — hold until comprehension review shows that visitors
do not read descriptive conditions as a disguised score or family diagnosis.

### `EXP-08 — The Return`

**Audience and job.** Any adult or parent after a form, conversation, decision,
test, boundary, or ending has met reality. The job is to notice effects and
choose an appropriate Return without making publicity or usefulness mandatory.

**Governing terms and axioms.** `TERM-09 Return`, `TERM-02 Ground`, `TERM-06
Compass`; `AX-05`, `AX-10`, `AX-11`, `AX-12`; `PH-08`; exclusion `RT-11`.

**Intended insight.** Return is relationship, not applause. It may consist of
value, learning, repair, gratitude, feedback, privacy, or an honest ending.
Human dignity is not a reward for producing an effect.

**Invitation and action.** Look at one form that has already met something
beyond intention. Notice what changed in the maker, between people, and in the
material or context. Choose one fitting response and its privacy boundary.

**Minimal flow.**

1. Complete any that apply: “In me…,” “Between us…,” “In the material or
   situation….”
2. Name who or what was actually affected, using generic roles where possible.
3. Choose one Return: offer value, request specific feedback, acknowledge
   influence, learn, repair, thank, keep private, or end honestly.
4. Check consent, safety, ownership, and whether any harm requires action.
5. Complete the Return, make a bounded plan, or state that no further action is
   called for.

**User-authored output.** A Return note:

> What changed…  
> What relationship or effect matters…  
> What I will offer, learn, repair, keep private, or end…  
> What boundary I will keep…

**Opt-out and exit.** A visitor may keep the form private, skip self-disclosure,
choose an honest ending, decide there is no relevant Return, or leave. Privacy
does not excuse ignoring real effects on others.

**No-score rule.** No impact score, audience count, beneficiary count,
gratitude measure, visibility bonus, usefulness rank, or comparison of Returns.
Repair is not a penalty; privacy is not a lower tier.

**Misuse or failure mode.** The experience fails if it pressures publication,
self-sacrifice, usefulness, forgiveness, gratitude, or disclosure; treats
audience size as meaning; or lets “personal expression” erase consequences.

**Safety and privacy.** No upload or public sharing is required. The visitor is
prompted to remove names and private details. Sharing a child’s form requires
the responsible adult to protect privacy and the child’s meaningful agreement
where appropriate. Serious harm or risk moves to appropriate real-world help,
not a poetic exercise.

**Return and next path.** The Return is the output itself when completed. The
only default continuation is `EXP-09 Ground Landing`; revision, repair, or exit
remain equally valid.

**MVP tier.** `MVP-core` — it prevents the resource from ending at
self-expression, while preserving privacy and freedom from compulsory impact.

### `EXP-09 — Ground Landing`

**Audience and job.** Anyone who is finished, tired, interrupted, uncertain,
or ready to stop. The job is to close, pause, or leave a cycle without creating
a debt to continue.

**Governing terms and axioms.** `TERM-02 Ground`, `TERM-04 Lift`, `TERM-11
Remembering Flight`; `AX-06`, `AX-08`, `AX-12`; `META-03`, `PH-05`;
exclusion `RT-10`.

**Intended insight.** Ground is a full part of life, not a lower state or a
productivity technique. Rest, routine, care, help, refusal, and ending do not
need to promise a future Flight.

**Invitation and action.** Look only for what genuinely needs closing or care.
The visitor may put something away, keep an obligation, ask for help, rest,
name an unfinished part, end the cycle, or do nothing further.

**Minimal flow.**

1. Ask: “Is anything unsafe, owed, shared, or left where it burdens someone?”
2. Choose one necessary closure, repair, request for help, ordinary act of care,
   or no action.
3. Name the cycle `finished`, `paused`, `ended`, `undecided`, or in the
   visitor’s own words.
4. Complete: “Another cycle is not promised.” The visitor may skip the line.
5. Leave without a next-step prompt.

**User-authored output.** An optional landing sentence:

> This cycle is…  
> What needs care now is…  
> Nothing else is required from it today.

**Opt-out and exit.** The primary exit is simply leaving. No explanation,
artifact, promise, or completion action is required. If even the landing prompt
is too much, the visitor may close it immediately.

**No-score rule.** No recovery score, readiness measure, rest streak, completed
closure count, or reward for returning later. Paused and ended states do not
reduce access or status.

**Misuse or failure mode.** The experience fails if rest is sold as fuel for
greater output, closure becomes another checklist, unfinished work triggers
shame, or the resource automatically reactivates the visitor with a new Call.

**Safety and privacy.** The experience asks for the least information in the
atlas. It stores no reason for rest or refusal and asks for no health details.
If ordinary or professional help is needed, asking for it belongs to Ground;
the resource does not diagnose the need.

**Return and next path.** There is no default next path. A visitor may later
choose `EXP-02 Problem Finder` or another experience, but Ground Landing does
not schedule, recommend, or promise that return.

**MVP tier.** `MVP-core` — it is the ethical counterweight to activation and
must launch with, not after, the experiences that invite action.

### `EXP-10 — Borrowed Map, Living Compass`

**Audience and job.** An adult examining one inherited rule, expert method,
family script, professional route, or cultural expectation. A parent may use
it only to examine their own map. The job is to choose the map’s present place
without treating influence as contamination.

**Governing terms and axioms.** `TERM-03 Gravity`, `TERM-06 Compass`, `TERM-02
Ground`; `AX-04`, `AX-05`, `AX-06`, `AX-08`, `AX-12`; `PH-09`; exclusion
`RT-06`.

**Intended insight.** Maps can carry memory, craft, protection, and care. A map
becomes Gravity when it silently becomes the only possible sky. Authorship can
mean keeping, adapting, temporarily borrowing, or refusing a route.

**Invitation and action.** Bring one sentence beginning “A person like me
should….” Explore where it came from, what purpose it carries, what it obscures
now, who is affected, and which relationship to it is responsible for the
present moment.

**Minimal flow.**

1. Write the rule or route without naming other people.
2. Name the knowledge, care, protection, or purpose it may carry.
3. Name one present cost, blind spot, or question it makes hard to see.
4. Ask who is affected and which commitments, consent, or safety boundaries
   are real.
5. Choose `keep`, `adapt`, `borrow temporarily`, `refuse`, or `not decided`.
6. Write one condition for review rather than a permanent verdict.

**User-authored output.** A Map decision note containing the route, its carried
value, its present limit, the chosen relationship, affected commitments, and a
review condition.

**Opt-out and exit.** The visitor may use a low-stakes rule, skip its origin,
keep the map unchanged, remain undecided, discard the note, or leave. No one is
asked to disclose trauma or confront another person.

**No-score rule.** No autonomy index, conformity score, originality measure,
number of maps escaped, or preference for rejection over continuity.

**Misuse or failure mode.** The experience fails if all tradition,
relationships, expertise, discipline, or obligation are labelled Gravity;
introspection is treated as infallible truth; or the visitor is encouraged to
sever ties without consent, consequence, and support.

**Safety and privacy.** Prompts avoid names and identifying histories. Sensitive
origins may remain unnamed. High-stakes relationship, employment, legal,
financial, or safety decisions are not settled by this reflection and may need
appropriate advice or support.

**Return and next path.** The visitor may test one low-risk variation through
`EXP-04 Template Escape`, record the decision in `EXP-06 Flight Log`, land
through `EXP-09`, or leave the map in place.

**MVP tier.** `MVP-next` — it deepens the adult path but requires careful
language review so “unborrowed” is not heard as isolated self-sufficiency.

## 5. Cross-experience journey

The atlas is a set of open routes, not a course. A visitor enters by naming a
present job, not by taking a placement quiz.

### Entry without diagnosis

The resource may offer five self-chosen doorways:

- **A family moment feels stuck** → `EXP-01 What Grounds Flight?`
- **I need to say something honestly** → `EXP-03 Instruction or Invitation?`
- **I keep noticing something** → `EXP-02 Problem Finder`
- **AI is entering this work** → `EXP-05 Set the Wind`
- **I need to stop or land** → `EXP-09 Ground Landing`

These are links, not inferred recommendations. The visitor may open the full
atlas, choose another route, or leave.

### Suggested parent route

`What Grounds Flight? → Instruction or Invitation? → optional Creative Climate
Map → Flight Log → The Return → Ground Landing`

This route moves from the adult’s responsibility to honest language, one
situation’s conditions, a real encounter, relationship, and closure. The child
is never assessed, enrolled, or required to participate. Every arrow means
“may continue,” not “has unlocked.”

### Suggested adult route

`Problem Finder → optional Borrowed Map, Living Compass → optional Template
Escape → Flight Log → The Return → Ground Landing`

This route moves from attention to direction, one revisable form, encounter,
responsibility, and rest. A `no` or `not now` from Problem Finder may go
directly to Ground Landing or out of the resource.

### Wind as an overlay

`Set the Wind` may enter before Making, during revision, or before publication.
It is not a required stage and does not replace Problem Finder or Compass. Its
output may be carried into Flight Log only when the visitor chooses to copy it.

### The cycle does not own the visitor

The journey may start at Ground, Return, or a boundary rather than Call. It may
move backward. A visitor can revise the question, reduce the form, ask for
help, stop an unsafe encounter, repair a consequence, keep the work private,
or end without another Call. The resource never treats sequence completion as
evidence about the person.

## 6. Shared content and data model — conceptual

The later MVP specification needs a small common grammar. This is a content
model, not a database or technical architecture.

| Concept | Contains | Explicitly does not contain |
|---|---|---|
| **Experience definition** | ID, name, doorway, user job, governing terms, insight, prompts, output template, exits, guardrails, next-path options, tier | personality logic, diagnostic rules, ideal answer, conversion target |
| **Working reflection** | optional words the visitor writes for this one experience | inferred tags, sentiment judgment, hidden score, child profile, automatic recommendation |
| **User artifact** | an editable sentence, note, agreement, map, log, or ending authored by the visitor | certification, system verdict, ranking, prediction, compulsory public share |
| **Journey choice** | a visitor-selected next experience or exit | algorithmic placement, locked route, level, completion requirement |
| **Safety and privacy note** | context-specific plain-language boundaries and exit to real-world action | diagnosis, professional advice, collection of sensitive details |
| **Provenance link** | relevant `TERM-*`, `AX-*`, `META-*`, `PH-*`, and `RT-*` references | a claim that the experience is scientifically proven |

### Shared reflection vocabulary

Experiences may draw from these optional fields when relevant:

- `situation` — one observable moment in generic language;
- `call` — what was noticed and whether it is accepted, declined, delayed, or
  changed;
- `frame` — a real obligation, safety boundary, or condition;
- `open_part` — what the person or child can genuinely author;
- `compass` — purpose, consent, affected people, limits, and revision
  condition;
- `lift` — capacity, time, materials, skill, support, or need for Ground;
- `making` — the form, sentence, experiment, boundary, or request;
- `encounter` — what the form met;
- `feedback` — what answered back, without a verdict on the person;
- `wind_role` — AI’s bounded function and non-delegated decisions;
- `return` — value, learning, repair, gratitude, privacy, or honest ending;
- `ground` — closure, care, help, rest, pause, refusal, or no further action;
- `privacy_choice` — keep private, copy for oneself, share with a chosen person,
  or discard;
- `exit_choice` — continue, revise, pause, end, or leave undecided.

Fields are prompts, not required properties of a complete person or cycle. A
blank field carries no meaning.

### No child entity

The shared model deliberately has no `child`, `child_profile`, `family_score`,
`talent`, `potential`, `behaviour_history`, or similar entity. Parent
experiences record only the adult’s generic description of one situation and
the adult’s own frame, language, responsibility, or repair. They do not ask the
child to log in, submit work, answer a test, or surrender personal material.

The conceptual MVP should allow a visitor to copy, print, or discard an
artifact without creating an account or saved history. Any future proposal for
persistence, accounts, synchronization, sharing, or analytics requires a
separate privacy and product decision. This atlas does not authorize retention
of reflection content, and it never authorizes retention of children’s data.

## 7. Anti-gamification rules

The Flight metaphor must never become a game of altitude. The later product
specification must preserve these rules:

1. No points, scores, levels, ranks, badges, streaks, leaderboards, or
   certificates.
2. No altitude, speed, lift, distance, compass accuracy, or “wings unlocked” as
   a metric or reward.
3. No progress percentage across the worldview. The number of experiences
   opened or completed is not personal progress.
4. No coloured health grade, radar chart, readiness meter, personality type,
   parenting style, creativity index, or talent forecast.
5. No hidden weighting or total assembled from descriptive answers.
6. No locked content, advanced path, or better ending earned by completion.
7. No streak recovery, unfinished-task warning, shame-based reminder, or copy
   implying that the visitor has failed to fly.
8. No default comparison with peers, families, age groups, prior cycles, or AI.
9. No automatic recommendation inferred from writing, selections, time spent,
   or exit behaviour.
10. No public proof of participation and no sharing prompt framed as
    completion.
11. No reward for Return based on audience, impact, gratitude, or usefulness.
12. No penalty for privacy, help, refusal, Ground, or leaving the resource.

Descriptive words such as `paused`, `finished`, `kept private`, or `not now`
are permitted only when the visitor chooses them for the current artifact.
They are not stored as traits or compared over time.

## 8. Accessibility and language considerations

Accessibility is part of Ground, not an optional polish layer. The later MVP
specification should preserve the following content requirements:

- Every metaphor has a plain-language explanation. A visitor can use the
  practice without believing in or adopting Flight vocabulary.
- Prompts use short sentences, concrete verbs, and one decision at a time.
  Visitors can review the complete prompt set before answering.
- No step is timed. No meaning depends on motion, sound, colour, spatial
  dragging, hover, handwriting, or fine motor control.
- Every path is operable by keyboard and compatible with screen readers in the
  later implementation; headings and field purposes must remain explicit.
- A visitor may type, dictate using their own accessible tools, print, think
  without recording, or work with a trusted assistant. Help does not reduce
  authorship.
- Short answers, long answers, blank answers, and custom words are all valid.
  Fixed choices always include a way to decline or write a different response.
- Examples do not assume a nuclear family, a particular gender, paid creative
  work, abundant time, verbal fluency, or independent physical action.
- “Parent” may include a caregiver or responsible adult, while the ethical
  asymmetry remains clear: equal dignity does not erase adult responsibility.
- Parent experiences do not require a child to read, respond, or disclose.
- The stable terms `Call`, `Compass`, `Lift`, `Making`, `Flight`, `Return`,
  `Ground`, `Gravity`, and `Wind` are used consistently and never as test
  labels.
- English copy prefers `may`, `can`, `might`, and `you may decline`. `Must` is
  reserved for real safety, consent, privacy, and responsibility boundaries.
- Translation should preserve the practical distinction behind each metaphor,
  especially Ground versus Gravity and invitation versus command. Literal
  poetic equivalence is less important than a clear ordinary-language path.
- The four responses `yes`, `no`, `not now`, and `differently` should remain
  equally legible in every supported language.

An accessible static or printable version must remain conceptually complete;
no essential insight can depend on an animated or highly interactive form.

## 9. MVP selection recommendation

### Recommended first release set

The first documentary MVP specification should include seven experiences:

1. `EXP-01 What Grounds Flight?` — parent entry and adult responsibility;
2. `EXP-02 Problem Finder` — adult entry and right to decline;
3. `EXP-03 Instruction or Invitation?` — immediate parent-language artifact;
4. `EXP-05 Set the Wind` — the project’s distinctive AI boundary;
5. `EXP-06 Flight Log` — shared single-cycle memory;
6. `EXP-08 The Return` — effects, privacy, learning, and repair;
7. `EXP-09 Ground Landing` — closure without productivity debt.

This set gives parents and adults a clear doorway, includes the AI context,
and completes the ethical arc from Call or frame through Return to Ground. It
does not require a profile, account, saved history, embedded AI model, child
participation, or scoring system.

### Hold for the next content release

- `EXP-04 Template Escape` should follow comprehension review of the word
  “escape,” ensuring it does not demean routine or encourage indiscriminate
  rebellion.
- `EXP-07 Creative Climate Map` should follow a no-score comprehension review.
  It carries the highest risk of becoming a disguised family assessment.
- `EXP-10 Borrowed Map, Living Compass` should follow language review across
  varied cultural and relational contexts so authorship is not confused with
  solitary self-sufficiency.

These holds are editorial risk controls, not scientific validation gates.

### Acceptance conditions for the later MVP specification

Every selected experience must:

- work as a standalone invitation and as an optional part of a route;
- state the intended insight in ordinary language;
- ask for a concrete user action;
- produce one editable, user-authored result or a valid no-result exit;
- expose pause, refusal, privacy, and exit without penalty;
- name its misuse or failure mode;
- preserve `AX-12` and avoid every anti-gamification pattern in §7;
- collect no child data and create no child or family profile;
- offer Return or Ground without forcing another experience;
- remain usable in a complete static or printable form.
