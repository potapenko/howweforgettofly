# AI as Wind

## A human-first practice of amplified making

Generative AI can produce a plausible answer before a person has decided what
is worth asking. It can multiply options, accelerate craft, challenge a draft,
simulate a response, and carry a form farther than one maker could carry it
alone. These are real powers. They are not a purpose.

This project calls AI **Wind**.

Wind changes the conditions of movement. It can lift, resist, redirect, widen
the visible horizon, or create the sensation of motion while carrying someone
along a route they never chose. Wind is not the wings, the pilot, or the
Compass. It does not determine which destination is worthy, whose consent is
needed, what a person is worth, or who must answer for an adopted result.

The point is not to keep human work untouched by machines. Human authorship
has never been pure: people think with languages they did not invent, learn
from other people, inherit maps, borrow tools, receive care, and revise
themselves in relationship. **Human-first** does not mean “human alone” or
“human made every word first.” It means that purpose, consent, judgment,
adoption, and responsibility remain human assignments in this world.

This is an ethical interface, not a claim about the inner nature of every
possible machine. It does not predict which occupations, markets, or technical
abilities will change. No forecast is needed for its central law:

> A person’s worth does not rise through AI use, fall through AI use, or
> depend on refusing AI. Worth is not a performance comparison.

Forms can still be inaccurate, careless, derivative, harmful, or unfinished.
They can be criticised and may require repair. The dignity of the person who
made or adopted them is not the prize for producing a better form.

---

## 1. What Wind means

**Wind is an external amplifier that changes speed, options, resistance, or
reach without choosing a worthy destination or owning the consequences.**

The metaphor has five practical consequences.

1. **Wind is powerful.** Calling AI a tool must not make its influence
   invisible. An offered option can change the question, not merely answer it.
2. **Wind is not neutral.** Every system makes some routes easier to see,
   easier to express, or more likely to repeat through its material,
   interface, defaults, and constraints.
3. **Wind has no mandate.** Fluency, confidence, novelty, or speed does not
   turn an output into an instruction.
4. **Wind receives a role.** Before or during use, a person can name what the
   system is being asked to amplify.
5. **Wind does not inherit Return.** The person or institution that adopts a
   form remains answerable to the people and conditions it affects.

### What the metaphor does not mean

Wind is not a mystical force and AI is not an oracle. “Follow the Wind” is not
an instruction in this project. The image does not imply that all AI systems
behave alike, that every use has the same risk, or that a person is fully
independent of tools and relationships.

The metaphor also has a stopping point. Questions of privacy, safety, law,
professional duty, factual accuracy, or a child’s welfare require concrete
judgment and appropriate expertise. Poetry cannot provide consent, verify a
fact, or repair a consequence.

## 2. The human assignments

AI may contribute language, structure, comparison, variation, execution, and
critique. Under this framework, it is not assigned the following decisions.

### Human worth

No output, originality score, speed advantage, market use, technical skill,
rest, refusal, or use of assistance determines anyone’s value. AI does not
rank people, and people do not need a uniquely human competitive advantage to
deserve dignity.

### The worthy Call

A **Call** is a noticed mismatch, possibility, curiosity, or care that invites
a response without commanding one. AI can surface possible questions. It
cannot announce a person’s true purpose, a child’s destiny, or a moral mission
that must be obeyed.

### Consent

AI cannot consent for the maker, the audience, a collaborator, a child, or a
person represented in the material. A simulated reaction is not permission
from an affected person.

### Care

AI can help someone remember a need, prepare a difficult conversation, or
imagine a more accessible form. It cannot perform the human relationship of
care by generating the appearance of attention. The people responsible for a
choice still have to notice the particular person, listen, remain reachable,
and respond when the form has an effect.

### Compass

**Compass** is the human practice of choosing direction through attention,
values, consent, limits, consequences, and revision. AI can supply arguments
to Compass. It is not Compass and does not deliver a final moral verdict.

### Adoption

Generation is not adoption. A human adopts an output when they decide to use,
share, rely on, or act through it. That decision requires enough understanding
to accept, revise, limit, or reject the form.

### Responsibility and Return

Responsibility does not disappear into “the model said so.” **Return** is the
meeting with effects, feedback, relationships, repair, learning, or an honest
ending. AI can help examine that meeting; it cannot take the maker’s place in
it.

### A child’s identity

AI is not assigned the right to diagnose a child, score creativity, infer an
inner type, predict a life path, or turn behaviour into a destiny. Adults keep
responsibility for safety and care while the child keeps authorship wherever a
real choice exists.

## 3. Six roles for Wind

The same system can perform several roles, but a single encounter works better
when one role is named first. Naming the role is not perfect control. It makes
the intended relationship visible enough to review.

| Role | Useful contribution | Non-neutral pull | Decision that remains human |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Mirror** | Reflects a draft, summary, tension, or repeated pattern back to the maker. | A tidy reflection can erase ambiguity or make one interpretation feel definitive. | Whether the reflection is recognisable, what it leaves out, and whether it changes the Call. |
| **Generator** | Produces materially different options, examples, questions, structures, or forms. | Abundance can substitute browsing for choosing; familiar patterns may define the option set. | Which options deserve attention, what criteria matter, and whether to choose any. |
| **Interlocutor** | Asks questions, rehearses a conversation, or helps a person articulate an unfinished thought. | The dialogue may become an agreeable loop or give machine fluency the aura of understanding. | Which question is real, when another human is needed, and when to end the exchange. |
| **Critic** | Tests assumptions, identifies gaps, checks a form against stated criteria, or offers counterarguments. | Confident criticism can become a moral verdict, imported taste, or endless revision. | Which criteria are legitimate, which criticism to adopt, and when the form is ready enough to meet reality. |
| **Craft aid** | Helps edit, translate, restructure, format, prototype, calculate, or execute a chosen technique. | Friction can disappear before the maker learns which part of the craft matters to this attempt. | The intended effect, acceptable method, verification standard, and final adoption. |
| **Simulator** | Generates plausible questions, edge cases, scenarios, or audience reactions before real contact. | A plausible simulation can be mistaken for evidence, consent, lived experience, or actual feedback. | What must still be tested with reality and whose real voice must be heard. |

No role becomes a hidden pilot. If the system begins selecting the purpose,
criteria, audience, form, and standard of success at once, the role has
silently expanded. Stop, separate the decisions, and return them to Compass.

### Role prompts

These are ordinary-language starting scripts, not magic formulas.

**Mirror**

> Reflect my current position in no more than five points. Mark any
> interpretation you are adding. Do not tell me what my true purpose is. End
> with one question I can decline.

**Generator**

> Offer five materially different ways to answer this Call. State the
> assumption behind each. Include “do nothing now” as a valid option. Do not
> rank them until I give criteria.

**Interlocutor**

> Ask one clarifying question at a time. Help me distinguish what I observed,
> what I assumed, and what I care about. Do not turn my answer into a
> diagnosis or mission.

**Critic**

> Test this form against these criteria: [criteria]. Separate factual gaps,
> ethical concerns, craft choices, and personal taste. Suggest repairs without
> scoring me or the work.

**Craft aid**

> Help with this bounded craft operation: [operation]. Preserve these
> decisions: [decisions]. Flag any change that alters meaning, consent,
> attribution, or audience.

**Simulator**

> Generate several plausible reactions from the stated context. Label them as
> hypotheses, not evidence. Identify what only a real person, source, or
> real-world trial can answer.

## 4. Wind through the Flight cycle

The complete arc is:

**Call → Compass → Lift → Making → Flight → Return → Ground**

In plain language:

- **Call** is something noticed that may invite a response.
- **Compass** is the human practice of choosing direction, boundary, and
  responsibility.
- **Lift** is the changing set of conditions available for this attempt.
- **Making** gives the question a perceptible and revisable form.
- **Flight** begins when an adopted form meets reality.
- **Return** meets effects, feedback, relationship, learning, and repair.
- **Ground** is reality, limits, maintenance, rest, obligation, and the place
  where a cycle can end.

Wind is not an eighth stage. It can enter any stage as a force or instrument.
Each stage keeps a human question open.

| Stage | A legitimate role for AI | Wind Drift | Human act that closes the gap |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Call** | Surfaces mismatches, alternative questions, or neglected possibilities. | A generated goal arrives with the force of destiny or optimisation. | Restate the observation in one’s own words and answer yes, no, not now, or differently. |
| **Compass** | Supplies arguments, precedents, trade-offs, counterexamples, and questions. | The option most fluently defended becomes the “right” direction. | Name values, affected people, consent, limits, stop conditions, and final choice. |
| **Lift** | Reduces a technical barrier, offers scaffolding, or helps find a smaller attempt. | Speed is mistaken for readiness; privacy, access, energy, or skill limits disappear from view. | Choose a realistic scale, support, tool boundary, or Ground. |
| **Making** | Drafts, varies, edits, translates, structures, prototypes, or assists a technique. | Volume replaces intention; polish hides that the maker cannot explain the form. | Keep, change, or reject deliberately; preserve the learning or craft needed for this attempt. |
| **Flight** | Helps prepare a form for contact or anticipate questions. | Simulation, engagement, or generated praise is mistaken for meeting reality. | Put the adopted form into an appropriate real relationship, test, conversation, or action. |
| **Return** | Organises feedback, compares effects with intentions, or suggests repairs. | Metrics or synthetic reactions replace people who were actually affected. | Listen, verify, attribute, repair, learn, or end honestly. |
| **Ground** | Documents decisions, supports maintenance, or helps close the cycle. | Continuous generation makes rest feel like lost momentum. | Stop the system, restore conditions, fulfil obligations, archive or discard the form, and make no promise of another Call. |

An AI-assisted form becomes Flight only when a person adopts it and it meets
something outside the generation loop. A simulated audience is preparation,
not Return.

## 5. Human-first and AI-first patterns

“AI-first” here does not mean opening a tool early. A human-first process may
begin with an AI draft, and a person may make very little of the surface form
by hand. The distinction is where the governing decisions live.

| Moment | Human-first authorship | AI-first drift |
|---|---|---|
| Starting | A person names an observation, care, duty, or tentative question. | The system is asked what the person should want. |
| Role | One contribution is assigned and can be revised. | The system silently becomes planner, judge, maker, and approver. |
| Criteria | The person names or negotiates criteria and affected relationships. | Criteria are inferred from defaults, popularity, or machine confidence. |
| Options | Generated options remain candidates, including refusal and delay. | The visible option set becomes the limits of imagination. |
| Making | The person understands enough to select, alter, and explain the adopted form. | Polish is accepted because it is difficult to challenge. |
| Critique | Feedback is sorted by fact, ethics, craft, and taste. | Critique becomes a verdict about the maker. |
| Contact | Simulation prepares for a real encounter. | Simulation is treated as audience consent or evidence of effect. |
| Adoption | The person can say what was accepted, changed, rejected, and why. | “The AI made it” substitutes for a decision. |
| Return | Effects are met by the people and institutions responsible for use. | Responsibility is assigned to the tool after consequences appear. |
| Stopping | No AI, less AI, pause, and Ground remain valid. | Use becomes compulsory, or refusal becomes a badge of purity. |

Human-first is therefore compatible with extensive assistance. It is
incompatible with hidden purpose, hidden criteria, hidden adoption, and hidden
responsibility.

## 6. The Set the Wind protocol

This is a voluntary practice for a real task. It creates no score, type,
diagnosis, or proof of authorship. A person may stop, use another tool, ask
another human, do the work unaided, or leave the Call unanswered.

### Intention

Give AI a visible role before its fluency becomes direction.

### Step 1: name the Call without asking AI

Complete one sentence:

> I notice ________. I may want to respond because ________.

The sentence may end with “I do not want to answer this now.”

### Step 2: make a Compass note

Write only what matters for this attempt:

- Who may be affected?
- What consent or privacy boundary matters?
- What would make me stop or revise?
- Which decision must remain mine?
- What do I not know yet?

### Step 3: assign one role

Complete:

> For this pass, Wind will act as ________. I want it to amplify ________. It
> must not decide ________.

Choose Mirror, Generator, Interlocutor, Critic, Craft aid, Simulator, or a
similarly bounded role.

### Step 4: set the material boundary

Use only the information necessary for the task. Do not provide another
person’s private material merely because it would improve the answer. For
family use, prefer generic, fictionalised, or child-created material over a
profile of the child.

### Step 5: make one request

Ask for an output small enough to inspect. If the answer changes the purpose
or criteria, pause and return to Compass instead of continuing the generation
loop.

### Step 6: run the adoption pass

For each part that may enter the form, mark:

- **Accept:** I understand and choose this.
- **Change:** This is useful material, but not yet mine to adopt.
- **Reject:** This does not fit the Call, boundary, evidence, or relationship.
- **Verify:** This claim needs a real source, person, expert, test, or rule.

If none of these judgments is possible, the output is not ready for adoption.

### Step 7: meet reality

Choose an appropriate contact: a material test, a trusted reader, a
collaborator, an affected person, a reliable source, a professional review, or
the real environment in which the form must work. Match the contact to the
stakes.

### Step 8: Return and Ground

Record four short lines:

> Wind helped with ________.  
> I accepted, changed, or rejected ________.  
> The effect or uncertainty I must answer for is ________.  
> This cycle is complete, paused, or returned for repair.

### Boundary

The protocol does not make unsafe use safe, turn a generated statement into
evidence, or transfer professional responsibility. It may be skipped. A
person’s dignity is unchanged whether the protocol, AI, or the whole attempt
is refused.

## 7. The direction test

The hardest case is not a bad answer. It is an attractive direction.

Suppose AI says:

> Your strongest opportunity is to turn this private concern into a public
> platform. You should build an audience around it.

The proposal may be perceptive, manipulative, conventional, useful, or all
four in different proportions. Under this framework it is not a Call and not a
mandate. It is a generated route.

Repair it in six moves:

1. **Label it:** “This is an option produced by Wind.”
2. **Recover the observation:** What existed before the suggestion?
3. **Open alternatives:** What are quieter, smaller, private, shared, delayed,
   or no-action responses?
4. **Use Compass:** Who is affected? What desire is actually mine? What
   boundary, consent, cost, or duty matters?
5. **Choose explicitly:** Accept, change, reject, or leave undecided.
6. **Own the next contact:** If the route is adopted, responsibility belongs
   to the people taking it, not to the fluency that proposed it.

This is how Wind may inform direction without becoming a hidden pilot.

## 8. Families: keep the child out of the instrument panel

A child is not a data source from which a system should infer a creative type.
A parent is not required to use AI to prepare a child for an imagined future.
The family branch begins with a simpler commitment: the adult keeps safety,
privacy, time, materials, limits, and honest responsibility; the child keeps
authorship wherever a real choice exists.

### The family boundary

Do not ask AI to determine:

- whether a child is creative, gifted, lazy, resistant, mature, or destined
  for a particular path;
- what a child “really wants” from behaviour, messages, drawings, recordings,
  school work, health information, or family history;
- a psychological, educational, developmental, or moral diagnosis;
- a creativity score, personality type, risk forecast, career destiny, or
  comparative rank.

Do not place private child material into a system merely to obtain a more
personal answer. Use the minimum necessary context, respect the applicable
rules and consent, and choose a generic or no-AI version when the boundary is
unclear. This framework does not make claims about what any particular system
stores, infers, or guarantees.

### Legitimate family roles

AI may help an adult generate open-ended material ideas, simplify an
instruction, offer several ways to demonstrate a task, translate a
child-authored text with review, or suggest questions about the conditions the
adult controls.

Useful prompts include:

> Generate three materially different ways to invite work with these
> materials. Do not infer anything about a particular child. Keep refusal and
> a non-AI version possible.

> Help me separate the unavoidable boundary from the part the child can
> choose. The boundary is ________. Offer choices of method, order, material,
> or pace without pretending the obligation itself is optional.

> Give feedback only on this artefact and the criteria we name. Do not infer a
> trait, ability level, motivation, or future from it.

### A child-facing sequence

1. The adult names what is required and why, if anything is required.
2. The child is offered a real choice of question, method, pace, material, or
   form inside the safe boundary.
3. If AI is offered, its role is explained in ordinary language.
4. The child may choose a non-AI route where a real choice exists.
5. AI feedback remains feedback on a form, never a verdict on the child.
6. The adult, not the child or the system, retains the larger duty of safety
   and care.

Equal dignity does not erase unequal responsibility. Avoiding AI does not
excuse an adult from teaching, setting limits, or seeking qualified help when
needed.

## 9. An adult scene: assistance without self-erasure

An adult has an unfinished letter about a difficult change. They ask AI to
write it “better.” The result is graceful, decisive, and more certain than the
adult feels.

An AI-first response is to send the letter because it sounds competent. A
purity response is to delete it because any assistance would make the letter
false.

A human-first response is different:

1. Name Wind as Mirror or Craft aid.
2. Mark every sentence that claims a feeling, promise, fact, or boundary.
3. Keep only what the adult can truthfully adopt.
4. Restore uncertainty where certainty was generated rather than lived.
5. Ask whether the recipient’s consent, privacy, or right to respond changes
   the form.
6. Send, revise, delay, or do not send.
7. Meet the real response and repair any effect.

The goal is not a percentage of human-made words. It is a form the person can
recognise, explain, and answer for.

## 10. Twelve failure modes and their repairs

Failures belong to an encounter, not to a type of person. None of these
patterns diagnoses a user.

| # | Failure mode | What has drifted | Repair |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **AI proposes the direction.** A generated goal feels like a discovered purpose. | Wind has entered Call and Compass as pilot. | Label it a candidate route, recover the prior observation, open alternatives, consult affected people, and accept, change, reject, or defer. |
| 2 | **A parent asks AI to assess a child.** The output assigns talent, motivation, type, or destiny. | Feedback on conditions or a form has become judgment of a person. | Do not use the classification. Remove the diagnostic task; ask about adult-controlled conditions or specific artefact criteria without profiling the child. |
| 3 | **An adult publishes an unexamined output.** The form is polished but the publisher cannot explain or defend it. | Generation has been mistaken for adoption and Flight. | Pause publication; run Accept, Change, Reject, Verify; check sources and affected relationships; revise, attribute, or do not publish. |
| 4 | **Options become an avalanche.** More generation feels productive while no choice is made. | Volume has replaced intention and Compass. | Stop generating, restate the Call, choose two criteria, keep at most a small set, or return to Ground. |
| 5 | **The critic becomes a judge.** Machine feedback is read as a verdict on ability or worth. | Critique of a form has become a rank for the maker. | Separate fact, ethics, craft, and taste; choose legitimate criteria; seek human feedback where needed; reject the rank. |
| 6 | **Speed outruns consent.** A form is shared, transformed, or aimed at people before boundaries are checked. | Lift has been confused with permission. | Stop distribution, identify whose material or interests are involved, seek real consent where required, limit use, and repair consequences. |
| 7 | **Simulation replaces contact.** Plausible reactions are treated as evidence that an audience approves. | Preparation has been mistaken for Flight and Return. | Label simulation as hypothesis, meet a suitable real reader or environment, and revise from actual feedback. |
| 8 | **Completion replaces learning.** A craft aid performs the exact operation a person intended to practise. | The role optimised the product while defeating the chosen learning Call. | Decide whether the aim is learning or delivery; narrow assistance to hints, examples, or review, or consciously change the aim. |
| 9 | **One route becomes invisible normality.** Repeated outputs narrow style, assumptions, or represented lives. | Wind’s non-neutral pull has disappeared from view. | Name the pattern, vary sources and constraints, invite affected human perspectives, and keep the option to reject the generated frame. |
| 10 | **Responsibility is laundered.** “The AI said so” is used after a decision causes harm. | The adopter has tried to transfer Return to the instrument. | Identify who chose, approved, and acted; meet the effect; correct the form or process; do not use uncertainty about generation to evade repair. |
| 11 | **AI use becomes compulsory.** Refusal is treated as backwardness, fear, or failure to prepare. | A tool has become a rank and Forced Takeoff. | Restore a workable non-AI route where choice is real; judge the form by relevant criteria, never the person by tool use. |
| 12 | **Purity becomes a badge.** Unaided work is treated as morally superior or fully self-created. | Human-first has become a myth of isolated authorship. | Name teachers, tools, traditions, collaborators, and conditions; choose assistance by purpose and boundary, not by status. |

The repair is never “trust yourself instead.” Compass is not solitary
intuition. It may require collaborators, evidence, professional expertise,
affected people, traditions, or rules. The boundary is that these inputs inform
a revisable human decision rather than masquerade as an infallible voice.

## 11. Responsible adoption and attribution

Attribution says where material came from. Adoption says who chose to use it.
They are related, but neither substitutes for the other.

### The adoption test

Before an AI-assisted form enters a consequential setting, the adopter should
be able to answer:

1. What role did AI play?
2. What material contribution did it make?
3. What did I or we accept, alter, reject, or independently verify?
4. Which people, sources, rights, duties, or uncertainties remain relevant?
5. Who will respond if the form is wrong or causes harm?

If the answers are unavailable, reduce the stakes, seek review, revise the
form, or do not adopt it yet.

### Proportionate transparency

Transparency should serve a relationship, not become ritual paperwork.

- **Private exploration:** a short note about the role of Wind may be enough
  to preserve one’s own learning and decisions.
- **Collaborative work:** collaborators should know when generated material
  materially affects shared decisions, voice, evidence, credit, or risk.
- **Public or consequential work:** follow the relevant venue, legal,
  professional, and source rules; verify factual claims with appropriate
  sources; name material AI assistance and unresolved uncertainty when they
  matter to trust or responsibility.

An AI output is not a source merely because it contains a citation. A fluent
claim is not evidence. Verification should be proportionate to consequence,
and real expertise should be used where the setting requires it.

Attribution must also avoid false purity. A person should not claim sole
independence when a system, collaborator, source, or tradition made a material
contribution. At the same time, disclosure should not imply that AI adopted
the final purpose or bears the human consequence.

A compact note can be enough:

> AI was used as [role] for [material contribution]. I/we selected and revised
> [what was adopted], verified [what required checking], and remain
> responsible for [the use and its effects].

## 12. Craft without a race

AI can increase execution without making craft obsolete. Craft is not valuable
because it protects a human advantage. It is valuable when attention to
material, technique, structure, and revision helps a person make an intention
perceivable and meet reality more honestly.

Sometimes the responsible choice is to let AI perform a routine operation.
Sometimes the operation is the learning. Sometimes hand-making is a pleasure,
a relationship, an accessibility need, a tradition, a way of thinking, or
simply the chosen form. None of these choices establishes a higher class of
maker.

Ask:

- What part of the craft carries meaning in this attempt?
- What part is merely blocking contact?
- What must I understand to verify or revise the result?
- What do I want to learn even if assistance would be faster?
- Which assistance makes the form more accessible without stealing a real
  choice from someone else?

The answer may change from one cycle to the next. There is no universal
co-creation ratio and no flight score.

---

## 13. Scope: a philosophy, not a diagnosis or forecast

AI as Wind is a practical myth and normative framework. Its existence does not
wait for a study to prove that one prompt or role improves authorship. Such
questions can be investigated and the practices revised without suspending the
ethical commitments.

The framework does not diagnose dependence, creativity, judgment, parenting,
or development. It makes no current labour-market prediction and offers no
ranking of tools or models. Technical features, product rules, professional
standards, and laws can change; concrete use must check the conditions that
actually apply.

The AI-as-Wind metaphor is original to this project. The older Russian book
that helped inspire *How We Forget to Fly* predates the present generative-AI
context. This chapter is neither a translation of that book nor a claim about
its authors’ views.

## 14. The AI Covenant

We will use Wind without mistaking it for the sky.

1. **Worth is invariant.** A person’s dignity does not depend on using AI,
   refusing AI, outperforming it, or producing anything at all.
2. **Wind receives a role.** We will name what AI is being asked to amplify
   and notice when that role expands.
3. **No hidden pilot.** An offered route is not a destiny, command, or moral
   mandate.
4. **Compass remains human.** Purpose, consent, limits, judgment, revision, and
   responsibility stay with the people who adopt and enact a choice.
5. **Wind is not neutral.** We will examine what its options make visible,
   easy, likely, or absent without treating influence as authority.
6. **Children are not profiles.** We will not use AI to assign a child’s
   value, type, diagnosis, potential, or future.
7. **Adoption is explicit.** We will accept, change, reject, or verify rather
   than confuse generation with authorship.
8. **Simulation is not relationship.** We will seek real consent, evidence,
   contact, and feedback where they are required.
9. **Return cannot be outsourced.** We will meet effects, attribute material
   help, learn, repair, or end honestly.
10. **Ground remains open.** We may pause, work without AI, ask for human help,
    or leave a Call unanswered without shame.

Wind may change. The Covenant does not depend on its present strength. It
depends on the kind of relationship we choose to build around power:
amplification without worship, assistance without self-erasure, and making
that remains answerable to the world.
