# How We Forget to Fly

## A memorandum for human authorship

We live among maps. Families, schools, traditions, professions, markets, and
machines offer routes before we know how to ask where they lead. We need those
maps. They carry knowledge, memory, warning, and care. Yet a map becomes
dangerous when it quietly calls itself the sky—when a familiar answer replaces
the question, performance replaces authorship, and a measurable result
replaces the relationship between a person and what they are doing.

Generative AI has made this tension impossible to ignore. It can offer a
finished sentence, image, plan, or argument with extraordinary ease. But an
abundance of answers cannot decide what deserves an answer. It cannot give
consent, care for an affected person, choose a worthy direction, or inherit
responsibility for a route somebody adopts.

We call creative authorship **Flight**. Flight is not an artistic profession,
a permanent state, or a higher kind of person. It is an event: someone notices
what calls for a response, chooses a direction, gives that response a form,
lets it meet reality, and returns to the world with learning, value, repair,
or an honest ending.

No one is required to fly. No one earns dignity by flying. This memorandum is
an invitation to keep authorship possible—for children, for adults, and for
anyone living among more ready-made answers than one life can follow.

---

## Article 1 — Dignity comes before Flight

Human worth is not awarded for originality, usefulness, courage, income,
speed, visibility, or output. It is not reduced by routine, dependence, error,
rest, refusal, assistance, or the use of AI.

Flight names a particular act of authorship, never a human rank. A successful
form does not make its maker more human. An abandoned attempt does not make
anyone less. We may criticise a form, refuse an action, and require repair for
harm. The person’s dignity is not placed on the same scale.

There are no flying people above non-flying people. There are people, equal in
worth, meeting different conditions and making different choices.

## Article 2 — Authorship begins with a question

A **Call** may begin as curiosity, care, beauty, irritation, injustice, a
broken object, an awkward sentence, or something unfinished between people.
It does not arrive as a command from destiny. It asks: *Do I want and have the
capacity to answer this now?*

The valid answers are yes, no, not now, and differently.

Authorship begins when a person can notice what is asking for attention and
decide what relationship to have with it. A machine may generate a thousand
possible questions. A parent, teacher, employer, or community may offer an
important one. None becomes a person’s Call merely by being fluent, urgent,
profitable, or praised.

## Article 3 — A map is not the sky

We do not become authors by rejecting every influence. Language, technique,
tradition, instruction, and imitation are part of how a human life learns to
move. A good map can save time, prevent harm, and reveal a route we could not
see alone.

The trouble begins when one route presents itself as reality itself. A metric
becomes destiny. A role becomes identity. An expert becomes an oracle. A
template answers before anyone has noticed the problem.

Keep the map. Look up. Ask who drew it, what it protects, what it hides, and
whether this route can be consciously adopted, altered, or refused. We do not
need purity from influence. We need a living relationship with direction.

## Article 4 — Flight becomes real in action

An imagined possibility is precious, but Flight begins when a chosen response
takes a form that reality can answer. The form may be a sentence, object,
conversation, boundary, experiment, repair, arrangement, performance, or
decision to stop.

The arc is:

**Call → Compass → Lift → Making → Flight → Return → Ground**

We notice. We choose a direction and its limits. We ask what conditions are
actually available. We make one revisable form. The form meets material,
fact, place, or another person. We receive what happened. We return, repair,
learn, close, and land.

This is not a productivity pipeline. Every stage has a door backward or out.
A ten-minute cycle can be complete. A great identity that never meets reality
is not more real than one small honest attempt.

## Article 5 — Ground belongs to the journey

**Ground** is the body, time, money, material, craft, safety, care, obligation,
routine, resistance, maintenance, and rest that make a real attempt possible.
Ground is not the opposite of freedom and not a lower form of life. Without
it, Flight becomes fantasy supported by labour and limits someone has been
asked not to see.

**Gravity** is different. It is an unexamined pull that turns fear,
convenience, prestige, an old protection, or a borrowed route into an
unquestioned destiny. Not every limit is Gravity. Not every obligation is a
betrayal of self. The distinction appears through attention, conversation,
and the possibility of revision.

Rest does not need to earn its place by promising future output. A person may
remain on Ground without shame and without announcing another departure.

## Article 6 — Freedom needs a Compass

Freedom is not the absence of every boundary. It is the capacity to take part
in choosing a direction while seeing enough of its limits and effects to
answer for it.

**Compass** is the human practice of asking: Whose Call is this? Why does it
matter? Who will be affected? Is there consent? What must remain safe? What
would make us revise or stop? What responsibility follows if we continue?

Compass is not infallible intuition. It can use evidence, teachers, laws,
traditions, collaborators, professional help, and AI. It does not make the
individual sovereign over everyone else. It keeps judgment visible,
relational, and revisable.

Freedom without care can become escape. Responsibility without choice can
become obedience. Compass holds the tension between them.

## Article 7 — Parents are Keepers of Conditions

A child is not a parent’s product, proof, future profession, or answer to an
uncertain age. The child is a person in the room now.

The parent’s role is not to manufacture wings. We call this role the
**Keeper of Conditions**. It holds safety, time, materials, rest, access to
craft, honest boundaries, conversation, and gradually increasing
responsibility. The adult
holds more power and therefore more duty. Equal dignity does not mean equal
responsibility.

Where a real choice exists, leave the child some authorship of the question,
method, pace, material, or form. Where safety or an unavoidable duty requires
instruction, name it honestly. A question is not an invitation if only yes
will be accepted.

Parents will sometimes control when they intended to care. The answer is not
parental shame or disappearance. It is attention, repair, and a more truthful
division between what the adult must hold and what the child can author.

## Article 8 — Adults can remember from the life they have

Remembering Flight is not a return to a pure childhood self. It does not
require a person to condemn their work, family, routines, or former choices,
nor to discover one hidden mission that makes every previous year meaningful.

Adults begin with the present body, present relationships, present skills,
present obligations, and the amount of energy actually available. Purpose is
not a buried object. It is a repeated relationship with the unfinished:
notice, choose, make, meet, return, and rest.

The authored act may be a public work. It may also be a private study, a
changed sentence, a request for help, a repaired promise, a necessary refusal,
or an honest ending. “Nothing today” remains a valid answer. Ordinary life is
not the waiting room for a real life.

## Article 9 — AI is Wind, not a hidden pilot

We call AI **Wind**: an external amplifier that can change speed, options,
resistance, craft, and reach. Wind can mirror a thought, generate alternatives,
test a form, simulate questions, or help with execution.

Wind is not neutral. Every system makes some routes easier to see and others
easier to miss. Its fluency can alter a question before we notice that the
question has changed.

Yet Wind is not wings, pilot, or Compass. Purpose, consent, judgment, care,
adoption, and responsibility remain human assignments in this world. Before
using AI, name its role and the decision it must not take. Afterward, choose
what to accept, change, verify, or reject.

Human-first does not mean human alone. People have always thought with other
people, inherited language, borrowed tools, and received help. It means no
hidden pilot and no purity myth. Using AI confers no rank; refusing it carries
no shame.

## Article 10 — Return completes the arc

A form is not finished merely because it has been produced or published.
**Return** begins when the maker meets what the form changed: the material
response, the other person’s answer, an unintended effect, a new fact, a need
for repair, or the recognition that the attempt should end.

Return may bring value, learning, gratitude, correction, apology, closure, or
a better question. It does not require an audience, a market, praise, scale,
or compulsory usefulness. A private form may remain private. Actual effects
on others still deserve attention.

Return is where freedom accepts relationship. Then the cycle lands on Ground,
not as defeat, but as completion.

## Article 11 — Refusal and help belong to authorship

No, not now, and differently are not defective versions of yes. Refusal may
protect a boundary, reveal that the Call belongs to someone else, recognise a
lack of Lift, or simply end an optional invitation.

Asking for help does not dilute authorship. Help can provide safety, language,
craft, access, witness, care, or the capacity to continue. Sometimes the most
authored act is to stop interpreting a locked door as a private failure and
seek material, legal, social, medical, psychological, or professional support.

Some duties cannot be refused without consequence. Then we should name the
duty rather than disguise it as a freely chosen Call, and preserve whatever
choice remains in method, pace, support, or repair. The right to refuse is a
commitment to honest power, not an escape from relationship.

## Article 12 — The myth remains open

This project offers a myth, not a doctrine of superior people. It may give us
symbols, stories, repeated practices, and a language for living. It does not
require belief.

There is no guru, secret interpretation, initiation, certificate, flight
level, diagnosis, promise of genius, or path to salvation. No author, parent,
community, or machine receives the right to measure another person’s inner
height. Any term may be questioned, translated into ordinary language,
revised, or retired when it becomes an instrument of control.

Metaphor stops where concrete safety and professional care begin. The source
of an idea can be named without becoming an authority that ends inquiry.

An open myth does not ask to be obeyed. It helps people see, choose, make, and
remain free to leave.

## A covenant and an invitation

We will not ask people to earn the dignity they already possess.

We will keep maps useful by refusing to mistake them for the sky.

We will protect questions long enough for a person to choose a response.

We will give freedom a Compass, action a form, and consequence a Return.

We will let Ground include craft, care, help, limits, routine, and rest.

We will hold children safely without claiming ownership of who they must
become.

We will let adults begin from the life that exists.

We will use Wind without giving it a hidden cockpit.

We will make room for yes, no, not now, differently, and an honest ending.

Begin with one ordinary sentence:

> I notice ________. I may want to answer because ________.

Then ask what must remain safe, what help is available, and what smallest form
could meet reality. Make it only if the Call is yours. Let it be answered.
Return what is needed. Land.

If no Call is yours today, remain on Ground without apology. That, too, keeps
the sky open.
